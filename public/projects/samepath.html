Mobile Collaboration

SamePath

SamePath pairs classmates with compatible study partners and lightweight collaboration tools. I led product strategy, UX, and full-stack development to help university students build peer accountability without adding friction to their already busy schedules.

Role
Product Designer · Full Stack Engineer
Timeline
Spring 2024 · 10 Weeks
Stack
React Native · TypeScript · Flask · MongoDB
Status
Private Beta · On-campus pilots

Designing a gentler way to form study crews

Most matching tools overwhelm students with forms or generic chat rooms. SamePath takes a progressive-disclosure approach: quick onboarding, gentle nudges, and context-aware suggestions powered by live course data. My goal was to make the “find a study buddy” task feel as casual as texting a friend.

What shaped the solution

  • Virginia Tech students needed a safe, invite-first environment before sharing personal schedules.
  • Matching had to reflect real availability, not just shared courses, to be meaningful.
  • Mobile-first flows let students act on push notifications from anywhere on campus.

Key product flows

1. Schedule-aware onboarding

Students can search by CRN or pull from their SIS export. I added helpful empty states, auto-complete, and guardrails that explain why schedule information unlocks smarter recommendations.

2. Compatibility scoring

I built a TypeScript matching service that scores classmates by free-time overlap, location proximity, and preferred study formats. The UI breaks the score into digestible tags—“Morning sessions”, “Project sprint ready”, or “Weekly review”—so students understand why a match looks promising.

3. Lightweight rooms

Once a match is accepted, teams move into “rooms” with shared task lists, meet-up reminders, and quick prompts. Flask handles real-time notifications while MongoDB stores room state for fast retrieval.

Outcomes & next steps

  • 30+ students participated in early usability labs—90% felt the matching explanations increased trust.
  • Live notifications nudged 12 dormant rooms back into collaboration during midterms.
  • Faculty expressed interest in using study rooms as supplemental group forming for large lectures.

Next

I’m layering in analytics dashboards for mentors, iterating on ARIA patterns for accessibility, and preparing the matching service for more universities.
