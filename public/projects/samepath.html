SamePath

SamePath is a solo-built project that unlocks shared time for classmates. I designed and engineered the system—from the concept to the APIs—to help students see who shares their courses, where long breaks overlap, and when it’s worth catching a friend between classes.

Designing a gentler way to form study crews

Most matching tools overwhelm students with forms or generic chat rooms. SamePath takes a progressive-disclosure approach: quick onboarding, gentle nudges, and context-aware suggestions powered by live course data. My goal was to make the “find a study buddy” task feel as casual as texting a friend.

What shaped the solution

  • Students often miss out on friendships because they never realise a friend is in the same section or an alternate section they could swap into.
  • Coordinating free time is painful—nobody memorises everyone’s schedule, so long campus breaks get lost to scrolling solo.
  • Privacy matters: schedules only surface after a mutual connection, with easy controls to pause sharing.

Key product flows

1. Schedule-aware onboarding

Students can search by CRN or import their SIS export. Guardrails explain why schedule data fuels smarter matches, and every screen reinforces that only approved friends will ever see your timetable.

2. Compatibility scoring

A TypeScript service crunches each person’s calendar, detects overlapping windows, and ranks them using a priority list the user sets. If more than three overlaps exceed 30 minutes, SamePath surfaces the highest value slots first so decisions become effortless.

3. Mutual reveals & rooms

Azure-hosted Flask APIs power friend requests, mutual schedule reveals, and quick meetup planning. Privacy toggles let anyone pause sharing or remove a connection instantly so students stay in control.

Outcomes & next steps

  • Early usability sessions confirmed the overlap explanations helped classmates understand why a connection surfaced.
  • Push notifications nudge study groups to reconnect during busier weeks like midterms.
  • Pilots with commuters explore how to use shared breaks to keep students on campus rather than heading home.

Next

I’m layering in analytics dashboards for mentors, iterating on ARIA patterns for accessibility, and preparing the matching service for more universities.
