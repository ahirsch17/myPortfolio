A responsive leasing hub for a family-run business. I designed and engineered every page to guide visitors from property storytelling into tour bookings, confirmation emails, and leasing workflows.

Role
Product Designer · Frontend Developer
Timeline
Winter 2023 · 6 Weeks
Stack
HTML · CSS · JavaScript · Express.js · MySQL · Nodemailer · Flatpickr
Result
Live on Render with automated booking & email confirmations

Aligning stakeholders on a digital-first leasing experience

The Hirsch family needed a modern site that highlights premium rentals and makes it easy to schedule private tours. I translated their offline sales playbook into a guided digital journey that surfaces credibility, differentiators, and easy conversion moments on every page.

Goals

  • Highlight high-quality property photography without slowing the experience.
  • Capture leads directly into an email pipeline and flag urgent requests.
  • Give agents an intuitive way to update availability without digging through code.

Experience highlights

Responsive landing experience

The hero on website.html stretches full screen, pairing high-impact photography with an availability banner and primary CTA that jumps visitors directly into the tour scheduler. Every breakpoint keeps that conversion path above the fold.

Global navigation & brand consistency

A shared navbar.css powers the announcement ribbon, logo lockup, and “Apply Now” CTA across every page so key flows stay one click away whether you are on listings, story, or scheduling.

Property catalog with quick filters

properties.html lists units with photography, specs, pricing, and Google Maps links. Client-side filters for beds, baths, and availability let prospects drill into relevant inventory without waiting on a backend round trip.

Rich listing detail pages

Each property page—such as property-hv.html—packages descriptions, square footage, and scrolling galleries that showcase interiors and exteriors. The layout keeps lead capture CTAs persistent while guests explore the imagery.

Company story & trust signals

aboutUs.html introduces every team member with headshots, credentials, and roles, reinforcing the family-run, service-oriented brand that differentiates the firm from generic rental sites.

Automated scheduling & CRM handoff

  • Interactive tour scheduling: schedule.html pairs Flatpickr date picking with live time-slot availability from /api/available-times, hiding booked slots and confirming reservations inline.
  • Server-side booking protection: The Express API (server.js) guards against duplicate emails and overlapping date/time combinations before accepting a reservation.
  • Automated confirmation emails: Successful bookings trigger templated Gmail messages to the guest and leasing inbox with visit details and a one-click manage link.
  • Self-service cancellation portal: Emails include a unique cancel ID; view-cancel.html fetches booking data via /api/booking and immediately calls /cancel/:id to release the slot.
  • MySQL-backed storage: When the remote database is online the system persists reservations, exposes /api/bookings for staff review, and enforces the same cancellation workflow from a single source of truth.

Applications & contact intake

Lead capture extends beyond tours. apply.html collects detailed applicant profiles—move-in date, lease duration, occupants, and notes—and posts them to /api/application, which emails both the applicant and leasing staff a formatted summary. contact.html already mirrors the Node mailer pattern, ready to integrate into the existing pipeline for general inquiries.

Visual language

Gradients and panel treatments that support premium positioning while staying performant.

Outcomes & next steps

  • Automated confirmations ensure new leads receive same-day responses without manual follow-up.
  • The guided scheduler gives prospects a clear path from browsing to booking a tour.
  • Reusable content modules make it easy for agents to update availability without touching code.

Next

Roadmap items include integrating the contact form into the email workflow, adding a lightweight CMS for seasonal promotions, and exploring subtle WebGL flourishes that preserve current Lighthouse scores.
