Marketing Website

RealEstateHirsch

A responsive marketing site for a family-run leasing business. Built to modernize property storytelling, simplify tour scheduling, and streamline lead capture without paying for heavyweight CMS tools.

Role
Product Designer · Frontend Developer
Timeline
Winter 2023 · 6 Weeks
Stack
HTML · CSS · JavaScript · Express.js
Result
Launched on Render with automated lead routing

Aligning stakeholders on a digital-first leasing experience

The Hirsch family needed a modern site that highlights premium rentals and makes it easy to schedule private tours. I translated their offline sales playbook into a guided digital journey that surfaces credibility, differentiators, and easy conversion moments on every page.

Goals

  • Highlight high-quality property photography without slowing the experience.
  • Capture leads directly into an email pipeline and flag urgent requests.
  • Give agents an intuitive way to update availability without digging through code.

Experience highlights

Tour-centric landing pages

I designed anchored layouts where each hero section pairs immersive photography with a single action—“Schedule a showing.” Scroll animations reveal floor plans, amenities, and neighborhood context without overwhelming mobile visitors.

Booking workflow

The Express backend proxies form submissions, normalizes them, and sends a structured email digest to agents. Conditional logic routes VIP properties to the owner and standard listings to junior agents, cutting manual triage time.

Content guardrails

To keep updates lightweight, I split property data into JSON files and micro-templates. Agents can update pricing and availability in a single source, and the site re-builds with fresh content during deployment.

Visual language

Gradients and panel treatments that support premium positioning while staying performant.

Outcomes & next steps

  • Cut average response time for new leads from 12 hours to under 4.
  • Increased completed tour requests by 32% within the first month after launch.
  • Agents now update availability in minutes without touching code.

Next

I’m experimenting with a lightweight CMS layer so seasonal promotions can be scheduled, and exploring WebGL flourishes for the gallery while preserving current Lighthouse scores.
